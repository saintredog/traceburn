# 1.6 — Competitive Analysis: Aura Gap Intelligence

**Project:** TraceBurn — Personal Data Exposure Scanner & Removal Tool
**Phase:** 1 (Research & Design)
**Method:** Customer review analysis — App Store, Google Play, BBB, Trustpilot, Reddit r/privacy

---

## Overview

Aura is one of the dominant consumer privacy/identity products, with broad marketing reach and significant revenue. Its customer reviews reveal a consistent and exploitable pattern: **the product sells the feeling of safety while systematically failing to deliver the substance of it.**

Each gap below represents a design requirement for TraceBurn. Where Aura extracts value from opacity and friction, TraceBurn must deliver transparency and directness.

---

## Gap 1 — No Proof of Action

### What Aura Does

Aura's dashboard shows counts of opt-outs submitted ("237 data broker opt-outs sent") with no supporting evidence. Users cannot verify whether any action was actually taken, which sites were contacted, or what the outcome was.

### Customer Evidence

> "They say they sent hundreds of opt-outs but I can still find myself on every site I check. How do I know they actually did anything? There's no receipt, no confirmation, nothing."

> "The number just keeps going up but my info is still everywhere. I think it's just a counter."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**Every opt-out submission generates a timestamped proof record:**
- UTC timestamp of form submission
- Encrypted screenshot of the confirmation page
- Extracted confirmation text (e.g., "Your request has been received")
- HTTP response code
- SHA-256 checksum of the screenshot for tamper-evidence

`traceburn proof --broker spokeo` displays the full audit trail. Reports export proof records as a verifiable PDF.

---

## Gap 2 — Cancellation Friction and Billing After Cancellation

### What Aura Does

Aura requires users to call a phone number to cancel — cancellation via the app or website is not available. Users report being charged for one to three additional months after cancellation. Disputes often require credit card chargebacks.

### Customer Evidence

> "Tried to cancel online — there is no cancel button. Had to call. They kept me on hold for 40 minutes then transferred me. Still got charged the next month."

> "They charged me for 3 months after I cancelled. I had to dispute with my credit card company. Absolute scam."

> "The only way to cancel is to call them. That's predatory. In 2024 you're telling me I can't cancel a digital subscription online?"

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**TraceBurn has no subscription, no billing, no account, and no cancellation process** because there is nothing to cancel. The tool is installed locally. "Stopping" TraceBurn means closing the terminal. Data stays on the user's machine. Financial lock-in is architecturally impossible.

---

## Gap 3 — Alert-Only Model

### What Aura Does

Aura's core function is notification: it alerts users that their data has been found somewhere. The majority of the "removal" work is either delayed, incomplete, or not performed at all. Users discover they have been paying for alerts, not removals.

### Customer Evidence

> "I realized after 6 months that all Aura does is tell me my info is out there. I knew that. I wanted it removed. Nothing was actually removed."

> "It's like a smoke alarm that doesn't call the fire department. Great, I know there's a fire. Now what?"

> "I kept waiting for results. My name and address are still on the same 12 sites they were on when I signed up."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**TraceBurn is an active removal engine, not an alert system.** Scanning and removal are the primary workflows:

1. `traceburn scan` — finds exposures
2. `traceburn remove` — initiates removal across all three tiers
3. Notifications are a secondary output of completed actions, not the product itself

Status display always shows: found, submitted, confirmed removed, re-verified.

---

## Gap 4 — Data Reappears After Removal

### What Aura Does

Data brokers routinely re-list personal information after an opt-out is processed. Aura does not systematically re-check brokers after submission, so users who re-search their own name discover their data has reappeared — sometimes within weeks.

### Customer Evidence

> "My information was gone for maybe 2 weeks then it showed back up on Spokeo. Aura never told me. I only found out by checking myself."

> "What's the point of removing it if it just comes back? They don't monitor for reappearance. You have to pay forever for something that doesn't work forever."

> "Took me 4 months to figure out Aura doesn't re-check after removal. That should be their entire product."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**Post-removal re-verification is a first-class scheduled workflow:**

- **T+7 days:** Re-scan broker for the specific user profile
- **T+30 days:** Full re-verification pass
- **T+90 days:** Long-term persistence check

If data reappears at any interval, TraceBurn automatically re-initiates the appropriate tier removal and notifies the user via the configured channel (terminal, Telegram). Re-verification history is stored and visible in reports.

## Gap 5 — Setup Complexity

### What Aura Does

Users report multi-hour setup processes, failed onboarding flows, and hours of support calls just to get the product functioning. Some report giving up entirely.

### Customer Evidence

> "Spent 3 hours on the phone with support before I could even log in. They kept blaming my phone. I'm an IT professional. Their onboarding is broken."

> "Setup took 2 days. Two. Days. For a privacy app. I still don't think it's working correctly."

> "I gave up after the third support call. I have no idea if it's protecting me or not."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**`traceburn init` completes full onboarding in under 5 minutes with zero support interaction:**

```
$ traceburn init
> Enter your name: John Smith
> Enter your email: john@example.com
> Enter additional addresses to scan (comma-separated, or press Enter to skip):
> Create vault passphrase: ••••••••••
> Confirm passphrase: ••••••••••

✓ Vault created and encrypted
✓ Broker database loaded (342 brokers)
✓ Playwright browsers installed
✓ Ready. Run `traceburn scan` to begin.

Setup complete in 3m 12s
```

No account creation, no email verification loop, no credit card, no support call.

## Gap 6 — International Users Excluded

### What Aura Does

Aura's product is US-centric. UK and EU users report being unable to cancel, unable to receive refunds, and finding the product non-functional (no broker coverage) for their region. GDPR rights are not surfaced.

### Customer Evidence

> "I'm in the UK. Aura took my money for 8 months. The product didn't work in the UK. I couldn't get a refund because I can't call a US phone number for free."

> "As an EU resident I have the legal right under GDPR Article 17 to request deletion. Aura doesn't help with this at all. They don't even mention it."

> "When I asked about cancelling from Australia they said 'we don't currently support international cancellations.' What?"

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**TraceBurn ships international support as a first-class feature:**

- `brokers.yaml` includes `region` tags: `US`, `EU`, `UK`, `global`
- GDPR Article 17 ("Right to Erasure") email templates built-in, ready to send
- UK ICO complaint template included for non-compliant brokers
- `traceburn scan --region EU` scopes to EU-relevant brokers
- No US phone number required for any function

## Gap 7 — Aggressive Upselling

### What Aura Does

Support agents use high-pressure sales tactics during service calls, including manufactured urgency ("this offer expires tonight") and upsell pitches mid-complaint-resolution.

### Customer Evidence

> "I called to cancel and they tried to sell me a more expensive plan four times. At one point the agent said 'this promotion literally ends at midnight.' It was 10am."

> "Every support interaction ends with a sales pitch. I called about a billing error. They tried to upsell me on family protection before they would fix it."

> "They held my cancellation hostage until I sat through a 15-minute sales presentation."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**TraceBurn has no upsell mechanism, no agents, no calls, and no financial relationship with the user.** The tool is open-source. There is no "premium tier" to upgrade to. There is no support phone number. Help is `traceburn --help`, the docs directory, and the project's GitHub issues.

---

## Gap 8 — Family Plan Silent Breakage

### What Aura Does

Aura family plans silently drop members who turn 18 without notifying anyone. Parents assume their children are still covered; they are not.

### Customer Evidence

> "My son turned 18 and Aura silently removed him from our family plan. No email. No notification. I only found out when I logged in 3 months later."

> "We had 6 people on our family plan. Two were removed without any warning. Aura's response was 'they aged out of the plan.' Nobody told us this was a thing."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**Multi-profile support with lifecycle event alerts:**

- Each profile in TraceBurn has a `profile_type` (`primary` | `family_member`) and optional `date_of_birth`
- If a family member profile has a birthday that changes their status, TraceBurn surfaces a notification: `[ALERT] Profile 'Emma' has turned 18. Review coverage settings.`
- No profile is ever silently modified or removed — all changes require explicit user action
- `traceburn profiles list` shows all profiles with status and last-scanned date

## Gap 9 — VPN Conflict and Feature Confusion

### What Aura Does

Aura bundles VPN, antivirus, identity monitoring, and data broker removal into a single product but fails to explain clearly which features do what. Users cannot tell what is working or why. Bundled VPN conflicts with other security tools.

### Customer Evidence

> "I have no idea what Aura is actually doing. The dashboard shows things happening but I don't understand any of it. What's the VPN for? Is it protecting me right now?"

> "Aura's VPN broke my work VPN. Support had no solution. Had to disable Aura entirely. So now I'm paying for nothing."

> "The alerts are constant and confusing. 'Dark web mention detected' — what does that mean? What should I do? The app just says 'we're on it' and nothing happens."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**Plain-language status display with zero jargon and no fake-urgent alerts:**

```
traceburn status

Profile: John Smith
Last scan: 2024-03-15 09:42 UTC (2 days ago)

Data broker exposures:
  ● 3 confirmed removed (proof on file)
  ● 1 removal pending — Spokeo (submitted 2024-03-15, re-check due 2024-03-22)
  ● 2 new exposures found — run `traceburn remove` to act

Next scheduled re-verification: 2024-03-22
```

- No score, no fear-based language, no vague warnings
- Every status item links to a specific, actionable command
- TraceBurn does not include VPN or antivirus — it does one thing and does it clearly

## Gap 10 — Refund Nightmare

### What Aura Does

Users who want refunds face months-long disputes, are redirected to third-party payment processors (Apple, Google), and frequently receive no refund despite qualifying under consumer protection law.

### Customer Evidence

> "Aura refused my refund. Told me to contact Google Pay. Google Pay told me to contact Aura. Went in circles for 3 months. Lost $180."

> "I cancelled within the 14-day refund window. They still wouldn't refund me. Had to file a chargeback."

> "The subscription renewed without notice and they denied the refund request same-day. Their refund policy is designed to be impossible."

### TraceBurn Design Requirement

**TraceBurn is open-source and free to use. There is no financial relationship to dispute, no refund to request, and no subscription to cancel.** Users who want enterprise support or custom broker additions can engage on their own terms. The base tool has zero financial lock-in by design.

## TraceBurn Design Principles

The ten gaps above translate directly into the following non-negotiable design principles:

1. **Proof over promises.** Every removal action produces a timestamped, encrypted, tamper-evident proof record. "We sent the opt-out" means nothing without evidence.

2. **No subscription, no billing, no account.** TraceBurn is a local tool. There is nothing to cancel, no recurring charge, and no financial relationship with the user.

3. **Active removal, not passive alerts.** Scanning finds exposures; removal eliminates them. Notifications are a side effect of completed actions, not the product.

4. **Re-verify, always.** Removal is only complete when post-removal re-verification confirms the data has not reappeared at 7, 30, and 90 days.

5. **Five-minute setup, zero support.** `traceburn init` must complete end-to-end onboarding in under five minutes without requiring documentation, support, or prior knowledge.

6. **International by default.** GDPR Article 17 templates, EU/UK broker coverage, and region-scoped scanning ship in v1. No user should be excluded by geography.

7. **No upsell, no agents, no calls.** TraceBurn is a CLI tool with no commercial support tier, no sales team, and no pressure. Help is documentation and open-source community.

8. **Explicit, never silent.** No profile, setting, or protection status changes without the user's explicit action or a clear notification. Lifecycle events surface as alerts, not silent failures.

9. **Plain language, zero jargon.** Every status message, alert, and report must be understandable by a non-technical user. No scores, no vague threats, no dark patterns.

10. **Open source, zero lock-in.** The tool is free, auditable, and runs on user-owned hardware. Trust is earned through transparency, not through a subscription that's hard to cancel.
